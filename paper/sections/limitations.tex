\section{Limitations}

This study is intentionally narrow. The main benchmark is a reduced MS MARCO-derived slice rather than the full passage ranking task, so the reported tradeoffs should be read as controlled experimental evidence rather than a final production ranking claim. Only one teacher backbone is used, and the teacher remains strongest in its zero-shot form. The student space is also small: query truncation, pooling, a single projection-head design, and one lightweight distillation formulation. Other compression strategies, stronger distillation schedules, or better student initialization schemes may behave differently.

The transfer evidence is similarly limited. SciFact is useful as a robustness check, but one BEIR dataset does not establish broad cross-domain behavior. Finally, the ANN study is secondary to the core encoder question. It shows that systems-side approximations can matter once the encoder is frozen, but it does not change the central conclusion that the main difficulty lies in shallow query representation quality.
